HeightsNext

“Simply put, “community building” means doing things that enhance a community. That’s exactly the aim of HeightsNEXT for the city of Columbia Heights, MN” HeightsNext.org

Background

HeightsNEXT is a sustainable community movement in Columbia Heights, MN. Public events introduce neighbors, network volunteers, and engage civic leaders. Working across organizations, HeightsNEXT promotes green technology, resilience, sustainability, justice, and equality.

My role

UX/UI Design.

The problem

HeightsNext’s mission is unclear and vague. There are many community events on the website but it is unclear how to get involved which ultimately hurts community involvement. 

The solution

Create more community involvement by making volunteering, donations and merchandising sections of the website more prominent to the website visitor.

User Research

We interviewed 4 people via zoom in order to find out how people interact with their communities, specifically how they give back to their communities. The questions were designed around donating and volunteering in local Nonprofits to help us understand what we need to prioritize in our redesign.

We surveyed 9 people. Demographics: 70% of our respondents are 32-39 years old, 30% are 40-51 .

Design Decision

While HeightsNext was geared towards community involvement and depended on community donations, buying merchandise and volunteering, the website did little to convey that message. With that in mind our we decided to redesign the website so that:

1

Make donations visible to the user at all time.

2

Bring out merchandise and give it more prominence in the homepage.

3

Make volunteering/Events a main feature on the homepage.
